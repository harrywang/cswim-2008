Exploring Service Research: Concepts, Experiences, and Directions

Abstract

The world is becoming a complex service system as marked with many recent advances in information technologies and business practices such as service-oriented architectures, software as a service, wholesale outsourcing of IT services, and offshoring of large data centers. During the past couple of years, researchers in industry and academia have started exploring new research directions concerning service science, service computing, service management, and service engineering. As a personal effort in this context, I organized two panels on service computing and management in AMCIS'07 and ICIS'07, respectively, leading to two articles recently published in the Communications of AIS, which can be downloaded at http://www.u.arizona.edu/~jlzhao/vita.html#JOURNAL.

Although there have been a number of recent publications exploring service research, this area is not yet well defined and has many open issues. As such, there is currently a great opportunity for new perspectives and new thoughts from various disciplines such as computer science, information technology, marketing, organization science, operations management, economics, and so on. In this talk, I will share my knowledge and personal views on service research. I will first go over some background material and basic concepts in service science, management, and engineering, then give an overview of several projects of mine related to service computing and management, and finally open a discussion on research opportunities in service research.

Speaker Bio: J. Leon Zhao is Eller Professor and Interim Head of the Department of MIS. He also holds a joint appointment in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Arizona. He taught previously at HKUST and College of William and Mary, respectively. He holds a Ph.D. and M.S. from Haas School of Business, UC Berkeley, M.S. from UC Davis, and B.S. from Beijing Institute of Agricultural Mechanization. He has published more than 120 research articles in major journal and conference outlets. He received an IBM Faculty Award in 2005 for his work in business process management and services computing. Leon has been associate editor of Information Systems Research, IEEE Transactions on Services Computing, and Decision Support Systems among others. He has co-edited nine special issues in various IS journals and chaired numerous conferences including the Workshop on E-Business (2003), the 15th Workshop on Information Technology and Systems (2005), the IEEE Conference on Services Computing (2006), and the First China Summer Workshop on Information Management (2007), the Arizona Exposium on Frontiers in Information Technology and Applications (2008), and the IEEE Symposium on Advanced Management of Information for Globalized Enterprises (2008).

Alibaba.com and The Development of China's E-commerce

Abstract

This presentation is to introduce the business profile, evolution and current status of Alibaba.com, a successful e-service company. The presentation will be focused on the B2B e-business, business model, and the development strategies of Alibaba.com using the relevant facts. Specifically, Specialization, Integration, Individuation, Precision, and Ecologization of the company will be addressed.

Speaker Bio: Mr. Chunxiao Liang is Senior Researcher and Vice President of Alibaba.com Corp, Vice Director of E-commerce Committee, China Information Economics Society, and advisory board member for e-commerce of National Development and Reform Commission, Ministry of Commerce, and Ministry of Science and Technology. Mr. Liang's long-term research interests are e-commerce, informatization and cyber-economy. Mr. Liang took part in many e-commerce, informatization and cyber-economy research projects, including the Development Plan of E-Commerce in the 11th National Five-Year Plan, Ministry of Commerce's Report of Chinese E-commerce, Chinese Academy of Engineering's Research on Modern Service Industry Informatization Strategy, Ministry of Science and Technology's Research on Chinese E-commerce Development Strategy and Research on Evaluation System of E-commerce Engineering, Research on Chinese Online-merchant Development, etc. Mr. Liang is also the author of the books: "E-commerce: from concept to practice", "Introduction to E-commerce", "E-commerce Application", "Talking about E-commerce" and "Search Revolution". Mr. Liang proposes some influential concepts and ideas, including e-commerce ecology, IT transition, portal evolution, search evolution, online-merchant evolution, e-commerce with commerce as the core, etc.
