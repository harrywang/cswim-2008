Business Intelligence Education: the Evolving MIS Program

Business intelligence (BI) has emerged as a valuable approach of information technology in finance, investment, marketing, human resource management, logistics and supply chain, quality control, and many other areas. Nowadays, the techniques and tools of BI are playing critical decision making roles in various organizations, owing to the evolution and growth of information systems. In one aspect, the fast accumulating information assets are urging the effective ideology and technology for information and knowledge processing. In another aspect, the success stories of BI stimulated growing demands for BI professionals and are challenging the MIS programs in the business school. This panel is to review the status of BI programs worldwide, identify the opportunities in BI education, clarify BI education objectives, curricula, and the program structure, and conceive the strategies of BI education. This panel is intended to provide a platform for the information exchange between educators and practitioners, and pave the way for further collaboration in BI education. The main topics of this panel include:

  • BI Job Market Trend. The job market for MIS professionals has been experiencing dramatic structural changes in the last decade. The panel is to discuss the demand of BI professionals from different angles and different areas. This will help identify the knowledge structure of BI professionals and provide the basis for the BI education program.
  • BI Program and Curriculum. The panel will review existing MIS programs and understand the relationship between the new BI program and current MIS program. The goal, objectives, curriculum of BI program will be discussed.
  • BI Tools. Currently, there are various kinds of BI tools available in the market, such as SAS Enterprise Miner, Text Miner, SPSS Clementine, WEKA, IBM DB Miner, SQL Server, ORACLE, etc. The panel will discuss the criteria for choosing a set of BI tools to maximize the effectiveness of teaching as well as to adapt to demands of the BI market. Specifically, the panel encourages the partnership between academia and industry in BI education.
  • BI Textbooks. The panel will discuss the best sell BI textbooks. A list of expected BI textbooks will be recommended.
